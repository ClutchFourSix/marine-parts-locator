Positioning and trust

A sourcing-first maritime parts platform.

Marine Parts Locator is designed to start as an efficient sourcing desk and evolve into a true marketplace once buyer demand, supplier coverage, and automated sourcing are in place.

Why this model works

Fragmented market. High search friction. Clear value.

Buyers

Often know what broke, but not the exact part number, compatible alternatives, or best supplier source.

Suppliers

Often have inventory, but inconsistent listing formats and limited discoverability across the wider market.

Platform

Can win by turning messy demand and messy supply into structured, searchable, ranked options.

Automation direction

The end state is AI-assisted sourcing, not manual brokerage.

The public site collects structured requests, while the next layer uses OpenAI tool calling and retrieval workflows to normalize requests, search approved sources, compare results, and draft an answer for the user.

Important note

Fitment should still be verified.

The platform should narrow the search, identify likely options, and surface compatible alternatives. Technical verification before purchase or installation still matters, especially across older vessels, retrofits, and salvage inventory.

Built to validate demand first, then automate sourcing and grow into a larger maritime network.
